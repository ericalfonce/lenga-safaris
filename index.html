Why Choose Tanzania?

Tanzania is not only the best second safari destination in East Africa but also one of the most irresistible African safari destinations to visit. With over 16 safari parks, it offers unmatched diversity for travelers. From pleasant climate and stunning natural scenery to extraordinary wildlife and warm-hearted residents, Tanzania promises a memorable experience.

With over a hundred ethnic groups, Tanzania also offers a rich cultural experience. You’ll enjoy unique cuisines, traditional dances, local rituals, and more. While you can explore on your own, a trusted Tanzania tours and travel company like Lenga Safaris ensures you get the most out of your adventure — including group excursions and authentic bush hunting experiences with local communities.

Why Go on Tanzania Tours

Tanzania feels like a real-life version of the Lion King, especially in Serengeti National Park — home to the largest lion population in the world. You’ll also witness the breathtaking Wildebeest Migration and experience the stunning biodiversity of the Ngorongoro Conservation Area, where elephants, cheetahs, and lions roam freely.

Mount Kilimanjaro, the "Roof of Africa", challenges climbers with its majestic height, while Zanzibar Island offers perfect relaxation with pristine beaches and romantic getaways.

Everything You Need to Know

Cost of a Tanzania Safari

Safari prices vary based on accommodation level, trip duration, destinations, transportation mode, and more. Luxury safari lodges may cost over $1500 per person per night, while budget-friendly options are also available.

Reduce costs by traveling in groups, choosing shorter trips, selecting easily connected destinations, and bargaining smartly. Remember, combining both Northern and Southern regions often involves flights, adding to the cost.

Where to Go

The Northern Circuit hosts top safari destinations: Serengeti, Ngorongoro, Lake Manyara, Arusha National Park, Mount Kilimanjaro, and Tarangire. Serengeti and Ngorongoro are must-visits and easiest to join.

Southern parks like Ruaha and Selous offer more affordable, less crowded safaris, although they are remote and often require flights to access.

Southern VS Northern Tanzania Safaris

Northern Tanzania has iconic attractions and a high tourist influx, making it pricier and more competitive. Southern Tanzania is more affordable, less crowded, but requires more travel logistics. Choose the North for highlights and shorter trips; go South for solitude and budget-friendly options.

Best Time to Go on a Safari in Tanzania

While Tanzania is a year-round destination, the dry season (June–October) is ideal for safaris. Witness the Great Wildebeest Migration between June and July or the calving season from January to February.

Birdwatchers will enjoy the wet season when bird activity peaks, especially in the Southern Circuit.

Flights VS 4x4 Safari Cars

Flights offer aerial views and save time between distant parks but can be expensive. Many still require 4x4 safari vehicles for game drives, so budget accordingly. Opt for flights if time is limited and budget allows.

Budget or Luxury Tanzania Safaris

With over 1.5 million tourists annually, Northern Tanzania is a premium safari region. Southern and Western circuits offer cheaper, less crowded options. Your choice of tour company also affects cost—some charge unreasonably high rates.

What you pack depends on your itinerary. A Kilimanjaro climb requires different gear than a wildlife safari. Once you book with us, we’ll guide you on the appropriate clothing and equipment for your adventure.

Tanzania Safari Guide

Everything you need to know before, during, and after your safari.
01

Before You Travel

  • VisaApply online at visa.go.tz or obtain on arrival. Most nationalities pay USD 50.
  • VaccinationsYellow fever certificate required if arriving from endemic countries. Malaria prophylaxis strongly recommended.
  • CurrencyTanzanian Shilling (TZS). US Dollars widely accepted. ATMs available in Arusha and major towns.
  • Travel InsuranceComprehensive cover including emergency medical evacuation is strongly advised.
02

In the Field

  • Departure TimesMorning game drives begin at 06:30 — pack a light jacket for cool bush dawns.
  • Wildlife EtiquetteRemain inside the vehicle at all times. Keep voices low and movements slow near animals.
  • PhotographyA 200–400mm telephoto lens is ideal. Golden hours — 6–8am and 4–6pm — produce the finest light.
  • ClothingNeutral tones only — khaki, olive, tan. Avoid white and bright colours in the field.
03

After the Safari

  • Zanzibar ExtensionA 45-minute flight from Arusha. Pristine beaches and spice tours make the perfect wind-down.
  • Stone TownA UNESCO World Heritage Site with rich Swahili-Arab history — ideal for a half-day walking tour.
  • DeparturesKilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) serves most international routes. Allow 3 hours before your flight.
  • MemoriesWe partner with professional wildlife photographers for post-safari prints and curated albums.
